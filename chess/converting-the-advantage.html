Converting the Advantage

Starting Position

We start with a pawn structure that likely arose out of a Sicilian Dragon. White has just captured a pawn on e4:

White trades into an endgame with equal material; after Qxe4 Rxe4 Bxa7, white wins back the pawn. This is the best line for white, but still the position is almost +2 for black. Why?
Let’s first look at the pawn structure. Blacks pawns are very well defended and in a solid formation. He has a central majority, and furthermore after the capture on a7, he has only one pawn that is on a dark square, meaning he has almost no pawn weaknesses for the enemy bishop to go after. White on the other hand has a 3-on-1 on the queenside, but it is not far advanced at all, and it is very hard for white to attack the weak b7 pawn.

Another reason why black is so favoured here: piece activity. The e4 rook is already keeping a watch on the weak f4 pawn, tying down the f1 rook to the defence, and now the f8 rook is ready to activate via rook a lift (with a tempo!) by sliding over to a8 and attacking the bishop. The bishop moves to b7 (where else) and black can activate his rook to the central ranks.
