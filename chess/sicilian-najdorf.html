Sicilian Najdorf

Opening

First 7 or so moves were pretty standard and I played them very fast - e4 c5 Nf3 d6 d4 cxd4 Nxd4 Nf6 Nc3 a6 Bg5 e6 f4 Be7 Qf3 Qc7 O-O-O:

Still theory, as the meme goes. Not knowing said theory, however, I set about blundering with the immediate b5??
The line here for white to take advantage of this is immensely complex, but the gist is that black is still not castled and so white can attack the centre in earnest, starting with e5 Bb7 Qg3!:

Here the top move recommended by the engine is actually Nbd7, giving up the f6 knight. Why? because if black tries to hang on to the material with say, Nh5, white can respond Qh4 Bxg5+ Qxg5 and the knight is trapped. If black still tries to cling on with g6 defending, white has a brilliant attacking sequence:

White starts with the brilliant double sacrifice Bxb5+!! Axb Nxe6!!

How can this work? After the recapture Fxe6 there is only one move that keeps the crushing evaluation for white - everything else loses. The key idea is that white can capture on b5 with tempo on the queen and in doing so has time. to jump into the incredible d6 outpost with check:

The only move that escapes forced mate here is to sacrifice the queen back in return with Qxd6. If black tries to run with Kf8, Rf1+ Kg7 Rf7+ Kg8 Qe7 leads to mate.
