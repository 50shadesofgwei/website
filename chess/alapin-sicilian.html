Alapin Sicilian

Position 1

Here as black I thought that playing Be7 and allowing the capture on c5 was bad because it would force me to move my bishop twice like in the QGD, and then white could argue that he won a tempo. The engine has a very different evaluation, saying that after Be7 dxc4 the move for black is actually just to castle:

And this position is marginally better for black. From here what I saw and was scared of was white playing b4 and hanging onto his extra pawn, but it turns out this is a huge blunder. It gives the following line: b4 Rd8 (notice the pressure on the bishop on d3) Bc2 Qxd1 Bxd1 a5!:

And black has good play against the odd piece placement and weak pawns on the queenside. Key idea: Nd5 … Nxb4 as the a3 pawn is pinned, opening the A file loses the A1 rook.

Position 2

Qh5 is an inaccuracy because of the defence Bd1, which I thought must be a positional concession from white (because why are all your pieces on the back rank / what is your knight on a3 doing), but the concrete nature of the position justifies it. White now has various discovered attack ideas, and the knight on a3, stupidly placed as it may appear, is actually pressuring my overextended pawns on c4 and b5.

The better plan here, and the one which I will have to fundamentally improve my understanding of these positions in order to conceive of and execute upon, is to slide the rooks over to the queenside and take advantage of the backwards c3 pawn and poor piece placement. The battery against g2 isn’t going anywhere, and there turned out to be no way to build the attack, so better to just leave it there as a looming, pressuring threat. I felt like a lot of my moves were very 1550ish this game, looking for attacks with insufficient material or positional justification, where the rest of my position is not in place to follow through.

I had focused, as is my habit, on the heuristic of his kingside seeming flimsy, but did not focus on the equally valid heuristic of ‘play where you have more space’. A common theme in my classical games is overestimating my capacity for attack, and starting off down such a line with insufficient ammo to follow it through. This is poor chess judgement on my part.
