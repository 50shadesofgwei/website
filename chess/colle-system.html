Opening

Standard D4 stuff: d4 Nf6 c4 d5 Nf3 Bg4?

Not the first time I’d faced this early Bg4 idea that day, the idea being I imagine to threaten the doubling of the pawns. Here I played e3, not allowing that, but there’s actually something way stronger: Ne5!

Because the e2 pawn has not yet moved, there’s no active pin along the d1-h5 diagonal. White can simply jump into e5 straight away, something he’s likely to do later in the middlegame anyway, and do so with a tempo against the misplaced g4 bishop. White’s position is comfortably better in the resulting game.
