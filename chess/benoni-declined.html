Benoni Declined

Opening

The opening was a variation of the Benoni where white declines to go into the modern variation with d5, but rather supports his centre with e3. By transposition, we end up in a line of the Caro-Kann Panov attack:

This is the first position where I spent any time considering what to do. Clearly, there is pressure on the d5 pawn, but I was reluctant to play e6 as my main goal in this position was to at some point break with e5. Questionable, given that the trading of the light squared bishop means that the dragon pawn structure is perfectly fine here. I thought for quite a while here and played Re8?!… e6 is clearly better. White castles, and I play e6 anyway because what else are you going to do; now however it looks somewhat silly as it blocks the e8 rook. Not at all conducive to the plan!

White responds with a3, and now I responded with a5, with the idea of preventing against the b4-b5 push. The cost of this move is that the black position now has a gaping hole on b5, a square to which the c3 knight can at any time jump in.
