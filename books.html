'After a lengthy consideration I admitted defeat as an engineer, and thought spitefully to myself: "Well, just let it drown!", and straight away the position did not appear to be so complicated. Now, I somehow realized that it was not possible to calculate all the variations, and that the sacrifice was, by its very nature, purely intuitive. And since it promised an interesting game, I could not refrain from making it. The following day, it was with great pleasure that I read in the paper how Mikhail Tal, after carefully thinking over the position for 40 minutes, made an accurately calculated piece sacrifice.'

- Mikhail Tal, The Life and Games of Mikhail Tal
